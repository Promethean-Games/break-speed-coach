Measure Your Break

Your phone's mic listens to the break. An audio engine detects the cue-ball strike and rack scatter, then calculates impact speed.

No hardware. No radar gun. Just your phone.

Important: Always use a tight rack for the most accurate and consistent readings.

Readings are speed estimates, not radar measurements. Use them as consistent training feedback.

◐
Phone Placement

Flat on the rail near the rack, mic side facing the player.

  • Same spot every session for consistent data
  • Stable surface only — never handheld
  • Don't touch the phone while recording
  • Record 3 breaks per session for the best read
●
Confidence Tiers

Every reading is scored on how cleanly the impact was detected.

● HIGH Cleanest read — trust this number
◐ NEAR-HIGH Very solid — use with context
◑ MEDIUM Rough estimate — interpret carefully
○ LOW Noisy read — retake if you can

Only HIGH-confidence breaks count toward your benchmark and Ideal Zone.

✓
Tag Your Outcomes

After each session, a quick tag sheet appears. Mark what actually happened on the table.

Scratch Cue ball in the pocket
Ball Pocketed At least one object ball dropped
Money Ball 8, 9, or 10 ball dropped on the break

These tags feed the coaching algorithm. The more you tag, the smarter it gets.
